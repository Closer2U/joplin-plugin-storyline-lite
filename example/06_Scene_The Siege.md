The siege began before dawn. Marcus saw the torches first — hundreds of them,
moving through the fog like fireflies made of malice.

Anna was beside him on the wall. She held the crown shard in one hand and his
hand in the other.

"If they breach the gate," she said, "take the shard and run. Promise me."

He looked at her. "I'm not leaving you."

"That's not what I asked."

Below, the battering ram struck the gate. The timbers groaned. The Obsidian Circlet
on the enemy commander's brow caught the torchlight and seemed to drink it in.
