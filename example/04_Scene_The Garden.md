The castle gardens were quiet in the afternoon drizzle. Anna found Marcus sitting on
the stone bench beneath the old oak, whittling a piece of driftwood into something
that might have been a bird.

"You're getting better at that," she said, sitting beside him.

He held up the carving. "It's supposed to be a hawk. Looks more like a potato."

She laughed — a real laugh, the first in days. He turned to look at her, and for a
moment neither of them spoke.

"When this is over," he said quietly, "I'd like to show you the coast. The real
coast, not the cliffs. There's a beach where the sand is almost white."

"When this is over," she echoed, not quite a promise.
